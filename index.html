Cyber-crooks don’t need you to “do something dumb.” They just need a single outdated plugin or an over-permissive browser extension. And because malicious code now rewrites itself with AI every few hours, the window to stop an infection before damage happens is smaller than ever.

The hard truth? A typical family photo library or freelance tax folder can be locked behind an unbreakable paywall in seconds. Let’s unpack why the default tools built into your devices can’t keep up—and what you can do today to stay two steps ahead.

Why You Still Need an Antivirus in 2025

Windows Defender is a solid first layer, yet recent independent testing shows it still misses up to 11% of zero-day threats during the crucial first hour of an outbreak. macOS isn’t bulletproof either—mac-focused malware jumped 28% this year alone, fueled by adware campaigns hiding in free creativity apps. And while iOS gets plenty of praise, the moment you sideload a profile or join public Wi-Fi, you create a bridge attackers can cross.

Meanwhile, Android sits in the crosshairs: over 65% of new apps flagged during Q1 carried hidden code for data theft or unauthorized billing. Even smart TVs are becoming launch pads for botnets. Attackers evolve faster than built-in defenses, weaponizing AI tools to mutate code signatures in minutes and slip past default shields. A dedicated security suite with live cloud analysis is now the only practical way to stay current.

Think of it like seatbelts in a car. Your vehicle ships with them―great. But for highway speeds you also want airbags, lane-keeping sensors, and automatic braking. Windows Defender is the seatbelt; a modern antivirus is the full safety package.
